Unity Video Tutorial

Video Goal

Video Link

Above is a link to a video tutorial that I created demonstrating features of Unity, a multipurpose computing platform used by universities and institutions. In my tutorial, I gave overviews of four different methods of transferring data files, with explanations of the features of each one. The methods included using Command tools such as rsync and scp, using the Unity OnDemand file browser (most intuitive in my experience), and using outside platforms including Globus and Filezilla. Overall, these methods are relatively user-friendly and allow users to transfer their files or data to different places, as well as save, edit, delete, and combine files for organization and efficiency and storage. Additionally, written instructions for these methods can be found on the Unity Documentation page linked here.

Below is a screenshot of the opening view of my video. When creating my video, I wanted to ensure a smooth process in not only creating the video, but also for the end product. I began by becoming as familiar as possible with the software, both by using it as well as practicing saying the correct terminology and using clear instructions while doing the voiceover. I wrote a rough script to follow while voicing the instructions to give brief explanations of the different methods, possible troubleshooting, and commentary on my experience of the platforms. During filming, I practiced navigating these platforms, and how to weave in my commentary to allow viewers to follow along clearly and without confusion.

Accessible Features

Tutorial videos to demonstrate these documentation methods and other content are an efficient and useful way to convey instructions visually and audibly for users who prefer a hands-on approach to using different platforms. When making instructional videos, an understanding of web accessibility and user experience (UX) is incredibly important to ensure clarity and ease for all users.

In terms of accessibility in my video, I tried to use labels of each method with a strong color contrast, while also looking visually appealing. I tried to make the video as organized as possible, with labels for each method, and doing one at a time. During the demonstration of each method, I tried to keep the screen recording as simple as possible to show an overview of the process without any distracting content on the screen.

Additionally, the video is accompanied by a transcript. This is a key component in video accessibility, for those who prefer to read written instructions, or have disabilities that may hinder their ability to watch or listen to the video. Personally, I like closed captions and transcripts when watching tutorial videos, so I ensured that the transcript was accurate and clear to ensure use for as many viewers as possible.

Scope of Video

The video was completed by using Sharepoint Stream and ClipChamp via Microsoft Suite, which ensured smooth sharing and access to the video clearly through a link. These platforms allowed me to submit and share my video with ease, and avoided excess complication when viewing the video.

Overall, creating this video not only deepened my understanding of using the Unity platform, but gave me the experience and confidence in making user tutorials that I am excited to bring to my future career. It also provided me with an understanding of how to view and use video tutorials when I am navigating different platforms and webpages to develop a deeper understanding of them and learn to convey these instructions to others.
